Principles of clear and effective UI messages

Why having voice and tone guidelines is important?

Voice and tone guidelines ensure that a company communicates consistently, effectively, and in a way that aligns with its brand identity. Creating a voice and tone guide helps:

  • Maintain a uniform brand voice across websites, apps, emails, and social media, making the company’s messaging recognizable.

  • Present the company’s personality, values, and mission, helping it stand out from competitors.

  • Provide a better user experience by using the right tone for different situations.

    • A friendly tone for casual interactions.

    • An empathetic tone for error messages.

    • A professional for formal communication.

  • Build credibility and emotional connection, making users more likely to engage and stay loyal to the brand.

  • Serve as a good reference for writers, marketers, and customer support teams, reducing guesswork and ensuring everyone communicates in a way that aligns with the brand.

How is voice different from tone?

Both voice and tone shape how a company communicates, but they serve different purposes.

Voice is the company’s identity. It reflects the brand’s values, mission, and personality. It should remain consistent across all content.

Examples:

  • A playful brand might always use a friendly, lighthearted voice.

  • A financial institution might have a professional, trustworthy voice.

Tone expresses the mood or feeling, which should change based on the audience and the situation.

Example:

  • A brand with a friendly voice might adjust its tone like this:

    • Success Message: "You did it! Your payment was successful." (Exciting)

    • Error Message: "Oops! Something went wrong. Try again or contact support." (Calm)

    • Security Alert: "We noticed an unusual login attempt. If this wasn’t you, reset your password immediately." (Serious)

Four voice and tone principles

The four voice and tone principles that many companies include in their guidelines are:

  • Clarity

  • Conciseness

  • Usefulness

  • Conversational tone
