Placeholders

The purpose of placeholders

Placeholders in UI are temporary text or visual cues written inside a field or content area, usually in a light share. The placeholder disappears when a user places the cursor or starts typing something in the field. The main purpose of creating placeholders is to guide users by providing tips on what to enter or what content will appear once data is available.

It’s important to remember that placeholders shouldn’t be long or too descriptive. They need to look as simple as possible and suggest to users that, for example, completing a form is an easy task. When placeholders are long blocks of text, users perceive them negatively because they don’t want to read all those words. Long sentences also suggest that a particular task may be complicated. Therefore, in forms and other areas where users need to complete forms, use as few words as possible.

Examples of placeholders

Placeholders can also take the form of questions, like this example from Airbnb. The question appears in the search box where you can enter your destination. This type of question encourages users even more to start planning their dream trip.

Another example of using a question as a placeholder comes from Fiverr. This question serves as a very practical and effective call to action and a promise that you can do everything with Fiverr. Moreover, users also know that the platform helps them find services.

Some placeholders inform users what they can find on the website. Defining some categories helps reduce the choice range and guides users on what they can enter in the field. For example, SoundCloud informs users that they can look for artists, bands, tracks, or podcasts on their website.

Sometimes longer placeholders are difficult to avoid, for example, when you need to explain something that is a helpful tip for users who might be struggling with completing a field. Recommendations and best practices

The examples above present various types of placeholders used across websites, platforms, and applications. Whether your placeholder will be a short question in the search box or a little bit longer sentence that gives a tip on how to complete a field, remember that the main purpose of placeholders is to provide hints to users. Don’t include placeholders just to fill up the space. Add them when there’s a good reason and they can really help users accomplish their goals.

The table below presents recommendations and best practices for the key aspects of placeholders.

Placeholders Recommendations and best practices
Body

  • Keep it short and clear.

  • Use examples for more complex fields.

  • Match the field’s purpose to the placeholder. Avoid repeating labels or headers in placeholders, as they don’t add any value.
Call to action (CTA)

  • Try question-based placeholders that can better engage users with a friendly, inviting question rather than raw text.

  • Avoid generic text like “Type here…”
Accessibility

  • Don’t use jargon or technical words.

  • Use high-contrast text to ensure readability.
